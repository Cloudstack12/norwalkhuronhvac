HOW TO MAINTAIN PROPER AIRFLOW IN YOUR HOME DURING A DUST STORM

During a dust storm, the air inside your home can become increasingly dusty and thick with pollen. This can make it difficult to breathe and stay healthy.

Here are some tips on how to maintain proper airflow in your home during a dust storm:

-Open all doors and windows as much as possible, especially if you have children or pets who are sensitive to allergens.

-Keep fans constantly running throughout the house (and turn them up high), unless you have an allergy that requires you to keep the fan off.

-If you need to sleep indoors, try sleeping in a completely closed room instead of an open one. The humidity will help reduce the number of dust particles that reach your throat/nose.

-If using electronic devices is important for you, try keeping them turned OFF when conditions outside are bad for visibility or health reasons.

The best way to maintain proper airflow in your home during a dust storm is to seal all cracks and openings that allow dust, dirt, and other particles inside. This will help keep the humidity levels low and minimize the likelihood of asthma attacks or other respiratory problems. You can also install ducts or filters to remove harmful allergens before they reach your indoor environment. Finally, make sure you have adequate supplies on hand (such as masks and talcum powder) in case of an emergency.

TIPS FOR CLEANING YOUR HOME IN ADVANCE OF A STORM

-Wash all windows and screens.

-Clean the air conditioning filters.

-Trim trees and branches that are overhanging your home or blocking sunlight/vents.

-Pump out water containers close to your home.

-Check for and fix any leaks.

-Empty the trash regularly, especially if your home is in a low area.

-Close all vents in the attic and on the roof.

-Remove all objects from the exterior of your home that could be swept away in a large windstorm.

When it comes to cleaning your home in advance of a storm, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First and foremost, make sure that all windows and doors are closed tight. This will help reduce the amount of airflow into the house, which can cause damage. Also, be sure to shut off electricity to essential appliances (like the refrigerator), so they won’t start up again during the storm and create more noise or messes. Finally, don’t forget to clean any surfaces that could potentially catch water (windowsills, porches, roofs).
